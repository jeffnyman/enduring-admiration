People's Computing

In the December issue (volume 1, issue 2) of PCC, there are two programs worth considering: Stars and Bagels. The first is quite simple: it's essentially a guessing game. The issue asks: "Is there a strategy that will guarantee always guessing the number in at most 6 guesses?" You can example of one of my playthroughs: 


        OK, I AM THINKING OF A NUMBER. START GUESSING.

        YOUR GUESS? 50
        *****

        YOUR GUESS? 40
        ****

        YOUR GUESS? 60
        *****

        YOUR GUESS? 55
        *******

By default, the game is designed to pick a number between 1 and 100 so here I tried a bisecting strategy to just start with a middle of the road number. I can see that I'm gaining more stars between 50 and 55 but I also have relatively few attempts left since the game limits you to seven guesses. 


        YOUR GUESS? 54
        **************************************************!!!

        YOU GOT IT IN 7 GUESSES!! LET'S PLAY AGAIN.
